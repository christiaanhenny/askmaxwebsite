What it remembers per person.

  • Inside language — isolated.

    “Manny” (your son’s family nickname) only when writing to your wife. Never leaks to your boss. Never leaks to your client. PortaVox stores per-contact vocabulary, not a global dictionary.

  • Multi-app aliases.

    Same person, different names per surface. PortaVox uses the right one automatically.

    SlackMaud van Leeuwen
    EmailMaud
    WhatsAppM
    LinkedInMaud van Leeuwen-Bakker
  • Facts about them.

    Their wife’s name. Their kid’s school. The deal they’re closing. The trip they’re back from. Tagged, persistent, surfaced when relevant — not buried in a chat history that resets every session.

  • What’s on their plate, with cooldowns.

    “Marathon next Sunday”. “Wife just got promoted”. “Renovation ongoing”. PortaVox surfaces these once, tracks whether you acknowledged, and waits the right amount of time before bringing it up again. Not nagging — cadence-aware.

  • Per-language preference.

    Auto-detects you write Dutch with Paolo, English with Marcus, German with Felix. Never asks again.

  • Per-contact project context.

    The deal memo you uploaded for this client. The brief you wrote for that prospect. Pulled into drafts where they’re relevant, ignored elsewhere.
