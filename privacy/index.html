Privacy & Security

Last updated: 28 May 2026

PortaVox is built around a simple principle: the model that knows your voice should not require us to know your voice. Your writing style, your contacts, your memory, your conversations — all of it stays encrypted on your device before anything reaches our servers. This page is the technical detail behind that promise.

What’s encrypted

Encrypted on your Mac before it syncs to our servers:

  • Writing styles and analysed patterns
  • Contacts and what PortaVox remembers about each one
  • Conversations (your paste history)
  • Memory facts (what PortaVox knows about you)
  • Chat sessions and messages
  • Project contexts and uploaded documents
  • User settings

How: Argon2id key derivation (64 MB memory, 3 iterations) combined with XChaCha20-Poly1305 authenticated encryption. The same primitives Bitwarden, 1Password, and Signal use. Your encryption passphrase is the only key. We genuinely cannot read your synced data — not us, not our database administrators, not a court order, not a breach. The ciphertext on our servers is opaque without your passphrase.

Where AI inference runs

When you press ⌥ Space, your request leaves your device for one inference call:

Primary model (text generation)

Google Vertex AI in the Netherlands (europe-west4 region). Google Cloud’s standard Data Processing Agreement auto-attaches to our account. Google does not train on API data and does not retain prompts or responses beyond what is necessary to serve the request.

Voice transcription, routing, and web search

OpenAI’s API handles three smaller paths: voice transcription (gpt-4o-mini-transcribe), the intent classifier that decides what action you want, and the optional web-search variant inside chat mode (gpt-4o-mini).

Three concrete safeguards apply here:

  • No training. OpenAI’s API terms exclude training on customer data by default — this is contractual, not opt-in.
  • No org-level logging. We have disabled all customer-facing API call logging at our OpenAI organisation: chat completions, audio transcriptions, and shared platform feedback are all set to Disabled. OpenAI does not retain these calls in our account’s accessible logs.
  • No Responses API retention. Our web-search calls hard-code store=false on the request, so OpenAI’s Responses API does not retain the prompt or response on its side.

OpenAI’s default abuse-monitoring retention (up to 30 days) still applies as a backstop — that is a platform-wide policy we cannot disable from the dashboard, only via formal Zero Data Retention enrolment which we will pursue at the appropriate usage tier.

Our edge functions do not log prompt content. Once your response streams back, the request is discarded.

What stays plaintext on our servers

We are honest about the full list of plaintext metadata we keep:

  • Subscription tier — for billing (Pro vs Free)
  • Rate-limit counters — incremented per request, no message content
  • Per-paste savings counter — anonymised count of how many generations you have made
  • Crash reports — stack traces only, no user content; sent with consent
  • Email address — for account login and beta communications

Everything else — the actual data PortaVox knows about you — is ciphertext-only on our servers.

Your encryption passphrase

Your passphrase is generated by you when you first set up PortaVox. We never see it. We have no recovery mechanism. If you lose your passphrase, your synced data on other devices stops being readable — treat it like a password-manager master password. We recommend writing it down in your password manager during setup.

If you change your passphrase, your encrypted data does not need to re-sync. We re-wrap the same data encryption key with the new passphrase-derived key — fast and bandwidth-free.

Your rights (GDPR)

You can:

  • Export all data we hold about you — email privacy@portavox.ai. We respond within 30 days.
  • Delete your account and all associated data — same address. Deletion within 30 days.
  • Correct inaccurate metadata — same channel.

For most of your data, deletion is immediate and local: signing out of PortaVox wipes the local cache on that device. Server-side ciphertext deletion follows your account-delete request.

Security disclosure

If you find a security issue, please email security@portavox.ai before public disclosure. We respond within 48 hours.

What we are not (yet)

We are honest about what we have not built yet, because vague claims invite the opposite of trust:

  • SOC 2 — planned for 2027. The architecture above is what carries the trust story today; SOC 2 is the formal audit of it later.
  • Customer-managed encryption keys — Vertex AI supports CMEK at the GCP layer, but PortaVox’s user data already uses user-controlled keys (your passphrase), which is the equivalent guarantee one layer up.
  • HIPAA / BAA — out of scope for V1. PortaVox is not intended for protected health information.
  • On-premise deployment — out of scope. Reach out if your use case requires it.
Questions? Email privacy@portavox.ai — we read every message.
