Why it can write you.

  1. 1
    Per-relationship voice. Greetings, sign-offs, sentence shape, even the language you switch to — learned per contact, not one tone for everyone.
  2. 2
    Inside language, isolated. “Manny” only when you write to your wife. “Boss” only with your co-founder. Never leaks across contacts.
  3. 3
    Facts about the person. Their wife’s name, their kid’s school, the role they just landed. Persistent, tagged, retrieved on every draft — not buried in a chat history.
  4. 4
    Phrases you killed. You crossed out “just circling back” twice. PortaVox never writes it again. Edits aren’t suggestions; they’re rules.
  5. 5
    The thread on screen. Reads what you’re replying to. Knows whether it’s a Slack DM (no greeting), a cold email (full structure), or a one-line iMessage. Picks shape automatically.
  6. 6
    Your project context. Pulls the exact line from the deal memo you uploaded for this client. Not a generic platitude — the actual sentence you wrote in the brief.
